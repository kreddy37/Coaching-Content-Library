üéØ Evaluation Guide: How to Choose Your Direction

As you explore each design direction, consider these criteria to determine which approach best fits your needs and preferences:

‚úÖ Layout Intuitiveness

Which information hierarchy matches your workflow? Does search feel like the natural starting point, or do you prefer filters front and center?

‚úÖ Visual Density

Do you want to see more drills at once (compact) or prefer generous spacing for easier scanning (spacious)? Consider your typical screen size.

‚úÖ Visual Weight

Should the interface feel calm and minimal, or bold and energetic? Which visual style represents your coaching brand and personality?

‚úÖ Search Prominence

How central should semantic search be? Hero element dominating the page, or integrated naturally alongside filters?

‚úÖ Filter Accessibility

Do you want filters always visible (sidebar/split), hidden until needed (dropdown), or minimal (pills)? How often will you use them?

‚úÖ Brand Alignment

Which direction best supports your emotional goals (confidence, trust, efficiency)? Does it feel professional enough for portfolio showcase?

‚úÖ Scalability

As your library grows to 100+ drills, which layout will still feel manageable and scannable? Which approach ages well?

‚úÖ Mobile Experience

Which direction translates best to mobile? Remember: you plan lessons on-the-go. Consider touch targets and screen real estate.
